# Camera and Framing

## Use The Camera When

- The viewer needs zoomed inspection.
- The scene contains dense geometry.
- A reveal needs a framing change to make sense.
- The camera itself is part of the storytelling.

## Prefer Static Camera When

- The layout can communicate the idea on its own.
- The camera move would distract from the concept.
- A simple re-layout is easier to read than a zoom.

## Camera Rules

- Move the camera slowly unless the motion itself is the point.
- Establish the full context before zooming in.
- Return to a wider view when the local detail is done.
- Avoid using camera movement to hide a crowded design.
