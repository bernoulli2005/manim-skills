# Camera Notes

## When To Use Camera Motion

- The scene is too large to read at one scale.
- The viewer must inspect a local detail.
- A zoom or pan makes the idea easier to understand.
- The camera itself is part of the explanation.

## When To Avoid Camera Motion

- The idea is already clear in a static layout.
- The move would distract from the core concept.
- The scene only needs a normal reveal or transform.

## Best Practices

- Keep camera movement smooth and deliberate.
- Let the camera support the visual rhythm.
- Use the widest useful framing first.
- Re-establish context after any zoomed segment.
