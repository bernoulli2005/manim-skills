# ManimCE Camera

## Overview

Use this skill for camera-driven scene design in ManimCE. It covers framing, zooming, panning, 3D camera motion, and when to keep the camera static.

## Use This Skill For

- `MovingCameraScene`
- `ThreeDScene` camera movement
- zooming into dense diagrams
- framing changes that support the narrative
- deciding when camera motion is unnecessary

## Rules

- Use the camera to clarify, not to compensate for a bad layout.
- Establish context before zooming in.
- Return to a wider view when the local detail is done.
- Keep camera motion slower than object motion unless the motion itself is the point.
- Prefer a re-layout over a camera move when the scene can be read more easily that way.

## Patterns

- Wide shot, zoom, detail work, return to overview.
- Static scene with one intentional camera move to emphasize a specific concept.
- Slow pan across a large diagram to keep the viewer oriented.
- 3D orbit only when depth and orientation are essential.

## Common Mistakes

- Using camera motion to hide crowded composition.
- Moving the camera too often.
- Starting zoomed-in before the viewer knows the full structure.
- Treating 3D camera motion as decoration instead of explanation.
